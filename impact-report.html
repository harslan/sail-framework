The Question

"In an AI-augmented world, what human capabilities become more valuable, not less?"

Every institution wrestles with this question. Most respond with tool training‚Äîteaching students to use ChatGPT, master prompt engineering, add AI to existing courses.

We took a different path. We built a framework around the one capability AI will never replace: judgment.

The Answer

The SAIL Framework

SAIL develops four interconnected competencies that together create the judgment AI cannot provide. Not an acronym‚Äîa compass for navigating an AI-transformed world.

S
Social Intelligence
Communicating about AI to colleagues, clients, and stakeholders
A
AI Literacy
Understanding AI's capabilities, limitations, and failure modes
I
Innovation/Inquiry
Questioning AI outputs; treating AI as thought partner, not oracle
L
Leadership
Taking responsibility for human-AI collaboration outcomes

SAIL in Action

Prompt AIChemy Competition

The centerpiece demonstration of SAIL principles. Students don't just use AI‚Äîthey become Strategic AI Directors, developing judgment through real business challenges.

üß™ What Happens in Prompt AIChemy Real Data

Students work with real business data‚Äîanalyzing Boston Airbnb listings, Brazilian e-commerce transactions, or other authentic datasets. They must:

  • Extract insights using AI tools strategically
  • Question AI outputs critically
  • Consider fairness and ethical implications
  • Defend their reasoning to judges

What Makes SAIL Unique

Seven Differentiators

Not another AI curriculum. A framework for developing the one capability AI will never replace.

1

We Named the Problem No One Else Has

Cognitive Debt ‚Äî Each time you accept AI output without evaluation, you accumulate debt. The debt compounds. When AI fails, you won't catch it. Other schools teach AI literacy. We prevent AI dependency.

2

We're Not Teaching Tools‚ÄîWe're Developing Judgment

Tools change every year. Judgment lasts a career. SAIL develops judgment for ANY AI system, not just today's tools.

3

The Elegance of SAIL

Not an acronym‚Äîa compass. Four pillars that actually mean something: Social Intelligence, AI Literacy, Innovation/Inquiry, Leadership.

4

Rooted in 2,500 Years of Wisdom

The Greek word kritikos means "able to discern"‚Äîthe perceptual capacity to see what matters. We're not reinventing critical thinking. We're restoring it for an AI age.

5

Built on Real Research, Not Buzzwords

MIT research on cognitive debt (Kosmyna et al., 2025). APA Delphi Report on critical thinking as "purposeful, self-regulatory judgment." 25+ years of learning science on contrasting cases.

6

We Don't Just Teach It‚ÄîWe Live It

Prompt AIChemy transforms students into Strategic AI Directors with real business data. Faculty AI Hackathons develop educators. SAIL Friday workshops build community.

7

The Promise That Cuts Through Noise

In a world of AI hype, one truth remains: "Skills can be automated. Judgment cannot."
