What Makes SAIL Different

Purdue talks about "AI competency." Berkeley Haas talks about "critical thinking" alongside creativity. Everyone lists critical thinking as another checkbox.

SAIL takes a different position:

Critical thinking isn't a fifth pillar. It's what makes the other four function.

The Greek word kritikos — the root of "critical" — means "able to discern." The APA Delphi Report defines critical thinking as "purposeful, self-regulatory judgment" — not just analysis, but judgment.

When we say SAIL develops judgment, we're recovering the full meaning of critical thinking and applying it to AI education. We're not teaching AI skills plus critical thinking. We're developing judgment — the integration of perception and analysis that tells you what to do when the answer isn't obvious.

Skills can be automated. Judgment cannot.

The Operating System

Critical thinking isn't a fifth pillar — it's what makes the other four function. Like an operating system that enables applications to run, judgment enables each SAIL competency to produce meaningful outcomes.

S
Social Intelligence
"How do we connect?"

Communicating AI insights to colleagues, clients, and stakeholders — making analysis actionable for different audiences.

A
AI Literacy
"How does it work?"

Understanding AI capabilities, limitations, and failure modes — knowing when different approaches work and why.

I
Innovation / Inquiry
"What's missing or wrong?"

Questioning AI outputs — the "Wait, that's weird" moments that separate strategic thinking from data regurgitation.

L
Leadership
"Should we do this?"

Taking responsibility for human-AI collaboration outcomes — ensuring recommendations are ethical and sustainable.

The Integration Effect

AI Literacy enables students to extract meaningful insights. Innovation/Inquiry helps them question and refine those insights. Social Intelligence makes insights actionable. Leadership ensures recommendations are sustainable. No single pillar works in isolation — they reinforce each other.
